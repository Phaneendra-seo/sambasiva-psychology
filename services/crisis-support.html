Types of Crisis

Recognize the signs and know when to seek immediate help for mental health emergencies.

Suicidal Thoughts

If you're thinking about ending your life, please reach out immediately. You deserve help and support.

Know the Signs

Crisis Warning Signs

Immediate Danger Signs

Talking about suicide or death
Looking for ways to harm yourself
Feeling trapped or hopeless
Saying goodbye to people

Emotional Warning Signs

Extreme mood swings
Rage, anger, or seeking revenge
Feeling numb or empty
Sleeping too much or too little

Behavioral Warning Signs

Giving away possessions
Withdrawing from friends/family
Increased substance use
Reckless or risky behavior

If you or someone you know shows these signs, don't wait. Reach out immediately for help.

For Concerned Others

How to Help Someone

1

Recognize the Signs

Learn to identify warning signs of mental health crisis in friends, family, or colleagues.

2

Start the Conversation

Express concern directly but gently. "I've noticed you seem to be struggling. Are you okay?"

3

Listen Without Judgment

Create a safe space for them to share. Don't argue or dismiss their feelings.

4

Encourage Professional Help

Offer to help them contact a mental health professional or crisis helpline.

5

Don't Leave Them Alone

If you believe they're in immediate danger, stay with them or ensure someone else is there.

6

Call for Emergency Help

If there's immediate danger of harm, call emergency services or a crisis helpline immediately.

If someone is in immediate danger:

  • Call 108 for emergency medical services
  • Call 100 for police assistance
  • Call crisis helpline: +91 98765 43210
  • Don't leave the person alone
  • Remove any means of self-harm if possible
